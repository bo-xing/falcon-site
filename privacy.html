Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Falcon (the “App”) handles your information. Falcon is a network throughput testing tool built on the open-source iperf, developed and published by an individual developer, Bo Xing.

Core Principle

The App does not collect, upload, or sell any of your personal information. There is no account system and no registration. The developer has no access to your device data. All data stays on your device.

Data the App Creates and Where It Is Stored

  • Test results and history: The results, charts, and notes for each test you run are stored only locally on your device (via iOS SwiftData / a local database). This data is never uploaded to the developer or any third party. You can delete it at any time within the App.
  • Test presets and target addresses: Saved test templates, frequently used addresses, and SSH peer information are stored only locally on your device.
  • SSH credentials: If you use the remote orchestration feature, any SSH password or private key you enter is stored only in the iOS system Keychain, protected by system encryption, and is never transmitted to the developer. It is sent directly from your device to the target device you specify, solely to establish the SSH connection when you initiate a remote test.

Network Connections

A network testing tool inherently generates network traffic. The App connects only when you initiate a test, and only to the target address you specify yourself (an IP / hostname / your own SSH peer device). The App does not perform any network communication in the background or without your action, and never sends data back to the developer.

Third Parties and Analytics

The App does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs. It does not track users and does not use cookies.

In-App Purchases and Payments

Pro features are offered through Apple In-App Purchase (StoreKit). Payments are processed by Apple under Apple’s privacy policy. The developer does not receive and cannot access your payment card details.

Children’s Privacy

The App is a general-purpose network utility, is not directed at children, and does not knowingly collect any information from children.

Data Deletion

Because all data is stored locally on your device, you can delete test history, targets, and credentials within the App. Uninstalling the App removes all of its locally stored data and Keychain entries.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy is updated, the latest version will be published on this page and the “Last updated” date above will be revised.
